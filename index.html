   Research Projects

Qualitative Study of Help-Seeking Behaviors on Social Media for Coping with Stressful Life Events

We explored how help-seeking behaviors on social media assist people under stress. By interviewing about 15 individuals, we aimed to understand their motivations for seeking advice online, their posting strategies and concerns, and their perceived value of received responses, shedding light on social media's role as a coping mechanism.

System Design to Explore the Potential of LLM-Enhanced Templates for Idea Reflection

Advances in LLMs provide rapid information for new ideas, but often lack structure and critical thinking. Hence, We designed and developed a FigJam plugin that combines LLMs with structured design templates to enhance precision in LLM prompts, organizes LLM responses for clarity, and facilitates critical reflection. An exploratory study confirmed the plugin's effectiveness in improving critical thought processes and idea reflection.

Quantitative Analysis of Social Support in Reddit Communities

Our study on r/mentalillness examined post flairs' effect on social support. Quantitative analysis shows post flairs don't consistently enhance social support, but specific flairs can influence it. The subreddit's atmosphere is overwhelmingly supportive, emphasizing the potential of online platforms in bolstering mental health support.

Exploring Different Social Roles of IoT Conversational Agents on User Experience

The Social Web of Things (SWoT) integrates the social web with IoT. Our study probed user perceptions of SWoT agent roles in smart homes: servant, friend, and parent. Following an experiment, results indicated a preference for the "friend" role due to emotional connection and efficiency. Users were cautious about sharing private matters and expressed interest in purchasing SWoT appliances if priced reasonably.
